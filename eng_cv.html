Motives
Looking back over the course of my career in information technology, I believe my primary mission has been to ensure the efficiency, appropriateness, and reliability of information delivery; not to mention the correctness of said information.

For most people in the industry this goes without saying; however a recent experience with a client reminded that some of my colleagues in the industry have lost the story where this mission is concerned. On the project team there were more than a few colleagues who still did not fully grasp the import of our "mission" as IT professionals; and through my actions, I made an effort to remind them.　

My introduction to the IT industry was in a temporary assignment at Goldman Sachs. I developed a monitoring system for the global risk group's margin account analysts. The system leveraged a real-time database to provide price movements of the collateralized securities. In the event that a given account's collateral value fell below the contractual limit, the system would alert the analyst and he/she would issue a "margin call" in order to secure the requisite funding.

The introduction of the system led to a change in how information was provided. Prior to the project's implementation, analysts were using printed paper reports. There were a large volume of securities to monitor and we would have to deliver four or five thick sheaves of printed reports to the risk group area. Naturally, this became unnecessary once the system went into production; and the effect was a many fold improvement in efficiency compared to the old way.

This resume itself was generated thanks to my serious stance with respect to how information is produced, consumed, and shared. As an information technology professional, I saw the need to develop (currently in-progress) a content management system that would allow me to flexibly answer a given head hunting agency's requirement to have my work history in a particular presentation format; there is no copying and pasting into WYSIWYG word-processing text editors.

Please refer to the following link to understand some of how I do this (Japanese only). Professionalism in IT to me means taking nothing for granted when information is concerned, not even the production of a resume.
