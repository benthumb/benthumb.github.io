Professional Interests

As a 24-year IT veteran with deep experience in both Japanese and Western business cultures, I’ve developed a unique perspective on technology's role in bridging organizational needs and cultural nuance. My philosophy is grounded in two core principles: use the right tool for the job, and become a better creator by being a discerning, enthusiastic software consumer.

Working with major Japanese financial institutions and global firms, I’ve seen how cultural values shape IT practices. Japan excels in hardware innovation and information curation, yet the overreliance on tools like Excel—what I call the “Excel Craftsman” culture—often stifles scalability and clarity. I advocate for purpose-built tools: relational databases like PostgreSQL for complex data, Visio Professional for clear documentation, and modern platforms that reduce technical debt.

My belief is simple: you can't build great software if you haven't used great software. Just as great writers read deeply, technologists must explore and evaluate top-tier tools. This mindset has fueled my success across diverse projects—from ServiceNow implementations and blockchain logistics to Olympic infrastructure and financial system modernization.

With experience in both Japan and the U.S., I bridge the visual, context-sensitive thinking prevalent in Japanese organizations with the Western emphasis on abstraction and separation of concerns. On a recent multi-year core banking overhaul at MUFG, I contributed as a project manager during the final phase of the initiative. I’ve also served as a business analyst auditing AML safeguards across a bank’s IT ecosystem—gaining insight into the intersection of regulatory needs and systemic design.

Ultimately, I believe great software design transcends cultural boundaries. By consuming technology thoughtfully and designing systems that honor the natural shape of information, I help teams move beyond legacy constraints toward true digital transformation.
